Puppy Nutrition & Diet

Balanced Diet Essentials

Puppies require a balanced diet rich in proteins, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals for proper growth and development. High-quality commercial puppy food formulated for their age and breed size is recommended.

Feeding Schedule by Age

8-12 weeks: 4 times daily
3-6 months: 3 times daily
6+ months: 2 times daily

Vaccination & Vet Care

Vaccination Timeline

6-8 weeks First puppy shots (DPV, DHPP)
10-12 weeks Second round of vaccination
14-16 weeks Third round + Rabies vaccine
Annual Boosters (1-3 years, then ongoing)

Core Vaccines

  • DHPP: Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvo, Parainfluenza
  • Rabies: Required by law in most regions
  • Bordetella: Kennel cough prevention
  • Leptospirosis: Important additional protection

Care Checklist

  • Regular vet visits (every 3-4 weeks until 4 months)
  • Deworming every 2-3 weeks until 12 weeks
  • Flea & tick prevention from 6-8 weeks
  • Heartworm prevention starting at 6-8 weeks
  • Spaying/neutering at 6-9 months

Warning Signs

Common Issues

  • Giardia & Coccidia: Intestinal parasites causing diarrhea
  • Kennel Cough: Contagious respiratory infection
  • Parvovirus: Serious viral infection (needs immediate care)
  • Ear Infections: Common in floppy-eared breeds
  • Hip Dysplasia: More common in large breeds
Contact vet immediately for: Severe vomiting/diarrhea, difficulty breathing, seizures, trauma, extreme pain

Grooming & Hygiene

Bathing Guidelines

  • Start early to build comfortable grooming habits
  • Bathe every 4-12 weeks (depends on breed/activity)
  • Use warm water and gentle puppy shampoo
  • Avoid getting water in ears and eyes
  • Dry thoroughly with towel or low-heat dryer

Coat & Nail Care

  • Short-haired: Brush 2-3 times weekly
  • Long-haired: Brush daily to prevent matting
  • Nails: Trim every 3-4 weeks
  • Ears: Check weekly, clean gently
  • Teeth: Brush daily with dog toothpaste

Dental Health

  • Start brushing at 8-12 weeks
  • Use only dog-specific toothpaste
  • Brush 3-5 times weekly (daily is ideal)
  • Provide dental chews and toys

Exercise & Mental Health

Age-Appropriate Exercise

8-12 weeks: 5-10 min sessions, several times daily
3-6 months: 15-20 min sessions, 2-3 times daily
6+ months: 30-60 min total daily (depends on breed)

Rule: 5 minutes per month of age, 2-3 times daily

Mental Stimulation Ideas

  • Puzzle toys & treat-dispensing toys
  • Training sessions (5-10 minutes daily)
  • Scent games and sniff walks
  • Interactive toys for problem-solving
  • Socialization with people & other dogs
  • Varied environments and experiences

Critical Socialization (3-16 weeks)

  • Expose to different people, animals, and sounds
  • Join puppy kindergarten classes
  • Create positive experiences during this window
  • Avoid traumatic experiences

Safety & Care Tips

Puppy-Proofing Checklist

  • Secure loose wires and cables
  • Remove toxic plants from accessible areas
  • Secure medications and cleaning supplies
  • Use baby gates to restrict areas
  • Remove choking hazards (small objects)
  • Provide safe sleeping spaces (crate/playpen)

Identification & Insurance

  • Get microchipped at 8-12 weeks
  • Register microchip with current info
  • Use collar with ID tag
  • Consider pet health insurance
  • Keep emergency fund for vet care

Building Good Vet Relationship

  • Find trusted vet early
  • Discuss breed-specific concerns
  • Keep detailed health records
  • Schedule regular preventive check-ups
  • Ask questions during visits
